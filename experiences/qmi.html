Co-op at the Quantum Matter Institute

My most recent co-op experience was at the Quantum Matter Institute at UBC, where I worked in an Ultra Fast Laser lab on a dual-comb spectroscopy experiment.

Tags:

Learnings Summary

From this co-op experience, I gained hands-on experience with the Vivado design suite, AMD Zynq SoCs, Python GUIs, and some introductory knowledge of laser/photonics setups.

The Project

For this dual-comb spectroscopy experiment, I was tasked with developing RTL for a Red Pitaya (Zynq SoC platform) in order to control the timing of trigger signals to an ablation laser and a data acquisition card (DAQ). There was also a software component to this project, as I was to develop a Python GUI that was able to control/configure the Red Pitaya as well as the data acquisition card.

The Hardware

The main goal in developing the hardware was to recreate the following timing diagram. Without getting into specifics, the idea was to take the “Delta Frep Signal” as a reference pulse and use it to generate the “Ablation Trigger Signal” and “DAQ Trigger Signal” pulses with a configurable delay that would be output from the Red Pitaya’s GPIO pins.

To achieve this, I custom wrote RTL and verified the results both in simulation (testbenches and Vivado’s simulator) and in real time by using an oscilloscope to view the signals.
